What is Eid and why do 2 billion people celebrate it?

Ramadan is the month in the Islamic calendar where people fast to commemorate the Prophet Muhammad PBUH (peace be upon him) receiving the revelations in the Quran. Eid al-Fitr is usually two to three days of celebrations at the end of Ramadan. The way Eid is celebrated varies all over the world, including: Donating to charities Making sweets and sharing Forgiving others Hanging lanterns Decorating with flowers Spending time with friends and family
